How TerraSight AI Analysis Works

TerraSight AI implements a robust non-destructive colorimetry technique using HSL (Hue, Saturation, Lightness) and HSV color spaces. Standard RGB representations of images vary greatly under changing sunlight and cloud cover. By converting leaf imagery into the HSV space, the algorithm isolates color components from lighting intensities.

Interpreting the Diagnostic Colors

Healthy Foliage (Green Mask)

Areas of the leaf with high chlorophyll concentration fitting the 25°–90° hue bounds. This indicates strong photosynthetic potential.

Chlorosis / Necrosis (Yellow/Brown Mask)

Foliage that has lost pigmentation or dried out (under 24° Hue). High ratios suggest nitrogen deficiency, iron chlorosis, root rot due to overwatering, or pest damage.

Unclassified (Darkened/Ignored)

Background items (soil, plant pots, shadows, margins) that do not meet the minimum Saturation (dull color) or Value (shadow) thresholds. This prevents noise in the final score.

Quick Calibration Tips

  • Avoid Shadows: Place the leaf in uniform, diffuse lighting. Heavy shadows might be flagged as soil/background noise.
  • Isolate Leaf: Try to capture just the leaf against a neutral background (like a white table or dark floor) to minimize false readings.
  • Adjust Saturation: If your plant has highly variegated leaves (e.g. stripes or yellow margins), raise the "Min Saturation" slider to focus strictly on pigmented parts.
  • Check Plant Profile: Ensure you are using the correct Plant Profile in the "Plant Library" page to get optimized diagnostic thresholds.
